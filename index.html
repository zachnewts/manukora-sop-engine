The Approach

Separate Math from Reasoning

LLMs are unreliable at arithmetic but excellent at inference, synthesis, and narrative. This tool pre-computes every metric in Python, then feeds enriched data to Claude. The model's job is to do what it's actually good at — interpret the numbers, make recommendations, and write a briefing a CEO can act on.

The key design decision

Don't ask the AI to calculate that MGO 263+ 500g has 2.49 months of cover. Tell it the cover is 2.49 months, that demand grew 23%, and that $37K/month in revenue is at stake. Then ask: what should we do about it?

What Gets Computed

Every number the AI references is pre-calculated and verified. The model never does arithmetic — it reasons on facts.

Demand trajectory. Bioactive Blends use M2→M4 (launched mid-Jan).

Prompt Engineering

The Prompt Is the Product

Two layers. The system prompt sets the analyst persona and output standards. The user prompt delivers a structured payload: business context, pre-computed data, and format instructions.

System Prompt

Sets the analyst persona and output standards — inference, not description. Includes a benchmark example of expected output quality.

 
You are a senior S&OP analyst at a premium DTC consumer brand
(Manukora — premium Manuka honey). You write monthly briefings
for the CEO.

Your output standards:
- Every recommendation MUST include: specific numbers, business
  impact in dollars, a clear action, and a priority rank.
- You do INFERENCE and JUDGMENT — not data description.
  Don't say "sales increased 23%." Say what that means for the
  business and what to do about it.
- When demand trends conflict with revenue materiality, flag the
  conflict explicitly and state your recommendation.
- Write for a CEO who has 5 minutes. Lead with what matters most.
- Use the pre-computed metrics provided — they are verified.
  Do not re-derive or round them differently.
- Only state specific timeframes if they can be directly derived
  from the data. Use months-of-cover as the unit of measure.
- Be direct. No hedging, no filler.

Example of expected output quality:
"MGO 263+ 500g has ~2.5 months of cover at current combined
sell-through, no stock on order, and demand has grown 23% over
4 months. At $54.99 retail and ~684 units/month combined, this
is ~$37K/month in revenue at risk. Cover will drop below target
before a new order could arrive — recommend ordering immediately.
Priority 1."

Match this level of specificity and decisiveness throughout.

User Prompt (Template)

Structured payload: reporting period, 6 business rules, full enriched data table (20 derived columns × 12 SKUs), and output format spec with per-section instructions.

 
Generate a monthly S&OP briefing for March 2026.

## Business Rules (critical — follow exactly)
1. Bioactive Blends launched mid-January 2026.
   M1 data is pre-launch. Trend is M2→M4 only.
2. Propolis Tincture 30ml phasing out Q2 2026.
   Deprioritize reorder unless cover < 30 days.
3. MGO 1700+ 100g has 3-month target cover
   (premium price + longer lead times).
4. Single pooled inventory for Shopify + Amazon.
5. Order_Arrival_Months = 0 means NO ORDER PLACED.
6. Use M4 as current rate. Note clear trends.

## Enriched Data (pre-computed, verified)
[Full table: 12 SKUs × 25 columns including
 combined demand, trend %, cover months, revenue
 at risk, priority scores, business flags]

## Output Format
1. Executive Summary (3-4 bullets, lead with action)
2. Sales Performance (trends, channel mix, launches)
3. Stock Cover Risk Assessment (sorted by severity)
4. Reorder Recommendations (min 3, ranked by
   revenue opportunity with full reasoning)
5. Trend Conflicts & Watch List
