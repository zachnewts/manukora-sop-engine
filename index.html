The Approach

Separate Math from Reasoning

LLMs are unreliable at arithmetic but excellent at inference, synthesis, and narrative. This tool pre-computes every metric in Python, then feeds enriched data to Claude. The model's job is to do what it's actually good at — interpret the numbers, make recommendations, and write a briefing a CEO can act on.

The key design decision

Don't ask the AI to calculate that MGO 263+ 500g has 2.49 months of cover. Tell it the cover is 2.49 months, that demand grew 23%, and that $37K/month in revenue is at stake. Then ask: what should we do about it?

Prompt Engineering

The Prompt Is the Product

Two layers. The system prompt sets the analyst persona and output standards. The user prompt delivers a structured payload: business context, pre-computed data, and format instructions.

System Prompt

Sets the analyst persona, output standards, and includes a benchmark example of expected reasoning quality.

 
You are a senior S&OP analyst at a premium DTC consumer brand (Manukora — premium
Manuka honey). You write monthly briefings for the CEO.

Your output standards:
- Every recommendation MUST include: specific numbers, business impact in dollars,
  a clear action, and a priority rank.
- You do INFERENCE and JUDGMENT — not data description. Don't say "sales increased
  23%." Say what that means for the business and what to do about it.
- When demand trends conflict with revenue materiality (e.g., a declining SKU still
  generating significant revenue), flag the conflict explicitly and state your
  recommendation.
- Write for a CEO who has 5 minutes. Lead with what matters most.
- Use the pre-computed metrics provided — they are verified. Do not re-derive or
  round them differently.
- Only state specific timeframes (days, weeks) if they can be directly derived from
  the monthly data provided. Do not fabricate or approximate timeframes beyond what
  the data supports. Use months-of-cover as the unit of measure.
- Be direct. No hedging, no "it's worth noting," no filler. State the situation,
  the implication, and the action.

Example of the output quality expected for reorder recommendations:
"MGO 263+ 500g has ~2.5 months of cover at current combined sell-through, no stock
on order, and demand has grown 23% over 4 months. At $54.99 retail and ~684
units/month combined, this is ~$37K/month in revenue at risk. Cover will drop below
target before a new order could arrive — recommend ordering immediately. Priority 1."

Match this level of specificity and decisiveness throughout.

User Prompt (Template)

Complete structured payload sent each run — reporting context, business rules, the full enriched data table, and output format spec.

 
Generate a monthly S&OP briefing for {report_month}.

## Reporting Context
- Period: M1 = December 2025, M2 = January 2026, M3 = February 2026,
  M4 = March 2026 (most recent)
- Report generated: {generation_date}
- Data source: Combined Shopify + Amazon sales, pooled inventory

## Business Rules (critical — follow these exactly)
1. Bioactive Blends (Immunity, Energy, Recovery) launched mid-January 2026.
   Their M1 data is pre-launch and meaningless. Trend is computed M2→M4 only.
   Flag them as new products.
2. Propolis Tincture 30ml is being phased out Q2 2026. Flag stockout risk but
   deprioritize for reorder unless cover is under 30 days.
3. MGO 1700+ 100g has a 3-month target cover (not 2) due to premium price and
   longer lead times.
4. Inventory pool: All Shopify + Amazon orders draw from ONE pooled inventory.
   Stock_On_Hand is global.
5. Order_Arrival_Months = 0 means NO ORDER PLACED, not immediate arrival.
   Only stock with Order_Arrival_Months > 0 is actually incoming.
6. Sell-through baseline: Use M4 (March 2026) as the current rate. Note clear
   trends if they change the reorder calculus.

## Enriched Data (all metrics pre-computed and verified)

[Full pandas DataFrame inserted here at runtime — 12 SKUs × 25 columns]
Each SKU row includes: combined_m1..m4, current_rate, trend_pct, trend_direction,
mom_m2_m1, mom_m3_m2, mom_m4_m3, Stock_On_Hand, arriving_stock, Units_On_Order,
Order_Arrival_Months, months_cover_raw, effective_cover, Target_Months_Cover,
cover_vs_target, Retail_Price_USD, monthly_revenue, revenue_at_risk,
reorder_priority_score, is_bioactive, is_phaseout

Column definitions:
- combined_m1..m4: Shopify + Amazon units sold per month
- current_rate: M4 combined (current monthly sell-through)
- trend_pct: % change M1→M4 (M2→M4 for Bioactive Blends)
- mom_*: month-over-month growth rates (%)
- months_cover_raw: Stock_On_Hand / current_rate (without incoming)
- effective_cover: (Stock_On_Hand + arriving_stock) / current_rate
- cover_vs_target: effective_cover - Target_Months_Cover (negative = below target)
- revenue_at_risk: monthly_revenue × shortfall months (dollars exposed if stock runs out)
- reorder_priority_score: revenue_at_risk × urgency_multiplier (composite ranking)

## Output Format

Structure the briefing with these sections in this order:

### Executive Summary
3-4 bullets maximum. Lead with the single most important action item. Include
headline wins, headline risks, and the top reorder action. A CEO should be able
to read just this section and know what to do.

### Sales Performance
What's hot, what's not. Channel mix observations (Shopify vs Amazon shifts).
Trend standouts — accelerating SKUs, decelerating SKUs, and any surprises.
For Bioactive Blends, assess the launch trajectory (M2→M4 only).

### Stock Cover Risk Assessment
Every SKU with effective cover below target, sorted by severity (largest negative
cover_vs_target first). For each: current cover, target, gap, incoming stock
status, and what happens if no action is taken. Include timeline context —
how many weeks/months until stockout at current rate.

### Reorder Recommendations
Minimum 3 SKUs, priority-ranked by revenue opportunity (not just cover risk).
For each recommendation, include:
- Current cover and trend
- Revenue at risk (dollars)
- Whether stock is on order
- Specific action (order now, expedite, etc.)
- Priority rank with reasoning

### Trend Conflicts & Watch List
- SKUs where declining demand conflicts with still-material revenue
- New products (Bioactive Blends) — early trajectory assessment and what to
  watch for next month
- Propolis phase-out status and any action needed
- Any other signals worth flagging for next month's review

Architecture Brief

Morning Intelligence Brief

Daily CEO Brief — Architecture

A daily automated pipeline that delivers a 2-minute narrative to the CEO at 6am local time, surfacing the 3 most important things about the business that day.

What It Is

Same pattern as the S&OP engine: pre-compute metrics from live APIs, score and rank signals by business impact, let Claude synthesize the top 3 into narrative. Runs daily, delivers to Slack.

5:30am NZT daily: Shopify Admin API ————┐ │ Amazon SP-API ———————├——→ [Signal Scorer] —→ Top 3 —→ [Claude] —→ Slack DM │ Gorgias API ————————┘ Each day's pull → stored in Supabase → becomes tomorrow's baseline

Data Sources & APIs

Shopify Admin REST API — commercial health and inventory.

EndpointWhat It Provides
GET /orders.json?created_at_min={yesterday}Order volume, revenue, AOV, discount usage, refund rate
GET /inventory_levels.jsonCurrent stock positions (diffed against previous day’s snapshot)

Amazon SP-API — the second revenue channel.

EndpointWhat It Provides
GET /reports (GET_FLAT_FILE_ALL_ORDERS_DATA_BY_LAST_UPDATE)Amazon order data, revenue, units
GET /fba/inventory/v1/summariesFBA inventory levels
GET /catalog/2022-04-01/items/{asin}BSR (Best Seller Rank) for key ASINs

Gorgias REST API — customer health and early warning signals.

EndpointWhat It Provides
GET /api/tickets?created_at_gte={yesterday}Ticket volume, new vs open vs resolved counts
GET /api/tickets?tags={tag}Trending issue categories (shipping, damage, product questions)
GET /api/satisfaction-surveysCSAT scores, sentiment shifts

All three APIs pull into a lightweight Supabase (Postgres) store. Each day’s pull becomes a row — trailing baselines for free.

Signal Scoring — How to Pick the Top 3

The pipeline generates ~12 candidate signals, scores each, and takes the top 3.

SignalSourceHow It’s Scored
Revenue / order anomalyShopify + Amazon$ deviation from same-weekday 4-week average
AOV shiftShopify$ change × order volume
Inventory threshold breachShopifyRevenue-at-risk for SKUs that crossed below target cover overnight
Amazon BSR / channel shiftSP-API + BothRank change × revenue weight; share shift 5%+ flagged
Refund rate spikeShopify% refund rate vs 30-day average × revenue exposure
CX ticket spike / issue surgeGorgiasVolume increase vs same-weekday average; any tag up 2x+ overnight
CSAT dropGorgiasRolling 7-day CSAT below threshold × ticket volume

Scoring: signal_score = abs(deviation_from_baseline) × monthly_revenue_weight. Revenue-weighted so a 10% drop on a top SKU outranks a 50% spike on a small one.

False positive prevention:

  1. Day-of-week normalization. Compare yesterday to same-weekday trailing 4-week average, not a simple 7-day mean.
  2. Diversity constraint. Cap 2 signals per category; ensure at least 2 categories in the top 3.

The Timezone Problem

6am NZ is roughly noon the previous day in LA. If Mike is in LA, a brief generated from NZ overnight data could sit unread for 16+ hours. A stale brief is worse than no brief.

Key design decision: Don’t delay a stale brief — regenerate with fresh data. The brief should reflect the most recent 24 hours relative to when Mike actually reads it, not when the cron job ran.

  1. 5:30am NZT: Pipeline runs. Brief generates. Delivered to Slack DM at 6am NZT.
  2. Read tracking: A lightweight check runs every 3 hours via Slack’s conversations.history API — has Mike viewed the message?
  3. If unread after 12 hours: Re-run the entire pipeline with current data. Deliver a fresh brief via Slack DM + SMS. This catches the LA morning scenario — Mike wakes up and gets a brief based on the last 24 hours, not yesterday’s NZ morning.
  4. On-demand: Mike types /brief in Slack at any time → immediate regeneration with live data. Handles the in-flight case.

Why this works: No location detection needed. Read status is the only signal. If he reads at 6am NZ, great. If not, he gets a fresh one at his local morning. Mid-flight? /brief on landing.
